## How each archetype description is structured

Every archetype includes:

1. **Profile line** — what they have, what's missing
2. **One-line truth** — the gut-punch sentence
3. **Score card** — High/Low per pillar (used in PDFs, not on public results page)
4. **Strength sentence** — one sentence acknowledging what they've already built. New. Sits between one-line truth and the description in the public results page. Also opens the PDF section for this archetype.
5. **Full description** — 3 paragraphs. Lives in the PDF.
6. **"You are someone who"** — 3 bullets. Public results page + PDF.
7. **The reframe** — single sentence. Public results page + PDF.
8. **What you need** — 1 paragraph. PDF only.
9. **CTA** — Public results page (opt-in for PDF) + PDF (Custom Action Plan or specific past newsletter).

## WRITING NOTES

**Tone across all 8:**

- Clinical enough to be credible. Human enough to feel personal.
- No wellness-speak. No "transform your life."
- Honest about the gap without making the person feel judged for it.
- Every low-tier result reads as a dignified, accurate mirror, not a consolation prize.

**The strength sentence is non-negotiable.**

Every archetype gets one. Every public results page leads with it (between the one-line truth and the bullets). It's the single most important addition. The page reads as a fair diagnosis (here's what you have, here's what's still being built) instead of a critique (here's what you're missing).

The Restarter's strength sentence does the hardest work. They scored Low on everything. The strength can't be a pillar they have. It's the underlying impulse: they're still trying. That impulse is what brought them to the quiz. It's the part that matters. Honor it without inflating it.

**The Practitioner is not a trophy:**

It's a real result some people will legitimately get. Don't write it as aspirational, write it as accurate. People who are Practitioners know it. The description should confirm, not flatter.

**Voice rules already applied:**

- Zero em dashes anywhere except the branded phrase "You never fail — you adjust" (not used in archetype descriptions)
- No "This isn't X. It's Y." patterns
- No "Not X. Y." patterns
- No forbidden transitions ("Here's the thing:", "Here's what's actually happening:", etc.)
- No AI tells (crucial, comprehensive, leverage, journey, etc.)
- Parenthetical clarifications used in place of em-dash interruptions
