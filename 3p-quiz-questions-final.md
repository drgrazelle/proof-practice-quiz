## PROOF

**Q1.**
A supplement is everywhere right now and a friend swears by it. What do you do?

- A. Try it. If someone I trust is seeing results, it's worth a shot.
- B. Research it thoroughly before spending any money.
- C. Look for actual studies, not testimonials, before deciding.
- D. Ask myself whether my fundamentals are solid first. A supplement probably isn't the gap.

---

**Q2.**
Two articles, same week. One says a food is protective. The other says it causes inflammation. Both cite studies. How do you decide?

- A. Go with what seems to work for my body. Research keeps changing anyway.
- B. Follow the most recent or largest study for now.
- C. Find sources without a financial stake and look at the overall evidence, not just one study.
- D. Focus on overall diet quality. One food rarely makes or breaks anything.

---

**Q3.**
A visibly healthy friend shares her exact health protocol and says it changed everything. Your honest reaction?

- A. Genuinely interested. If it's working for her, there's probably something to it.
- B. Curious but I'd want to understand why it works before trying it.
- C. Cautious. I'd want to know if it's actually supported by research.
- D. I'd wonder whether it's the protocol, or her sleep, food, and movement finally being solid underneath it.

---

**Q4.**
You're starting over after a long break. Where do you begin?

- A. Find a comprehensive program so I don't have to figure it out myself.
- B. Research the most effective current approaches before committing.
- C. Go back to basics. Sleep, food quality, movement. I know what the foundation is.
- D. Pick one sustainable thing and start there, even if it feels too simple.

---

## PRACTICE

**Q5.**
The week fell apart. Work, stress, no time. You missed everything. What happens next?

- A. Start fresh Monday when things settle down.
- B. Figure out what went wrong so I can plan better next time.
- C. Salvage something small before the week is over.
- D. Keep going at whatever level I can reach right now.

---

**Q6.**
You planned 45 minutes to exercise. You have 15. What do you do?

- A. Skip it. 15 minutes isn't worth disrupting the plan.
- B. Feel conflicted and end up not doing much while deciding.
- C. Do 15 minutes. It still counts.
- D. Figure out which part matters most right now and do that.

---

**Q7.**
Your life shifts significantly. New job, new baby, a move. The routine that was working no longer fits. What happens to your health habits?

- A. Put them on hold until things settle enough to do them properly.
- B. Design a new routine that fits before I start again.
- C. Find the smallest version of each habit that still counts and keep going.
- D. Adjust the approach to fit the new reality. The goal hasn't changed.

---

**Q8.**
Two weeks have passed. No crisis. Life just got busy and your habits quietly disappeared. How do you come back?

- A. Fresh start. New plan, new week, full commitment.
- B. Understand what caused the drift before trying again.
- C. Pick back up where I left off without making it a bigger deal than it is.
- D. Start with the easiest habit first, just to get back in motion.

---

## PURPOSE

**Q9.**
Three weeks in. The initial energy is completely gone. It's just hard now. What keeps you going?

- A. Honestly, not much. When motivation fades, things usually fade with it.
- B. I remind myself of my goal and try to push through.
- C. I reconnect with why I started. Something that matters more than the discomfort.
- D. I don't rely on motivation. I know what this is in service of.

---

**Q10.**
Someone genuinely asks why you prioritize your health. What's your honest answer?

- A. I'm not sure I could give a clear answer right now.
- B. I want to feel good, have more energy, and stay at a healthy weight.
- C. I want to be healthy enough to show up fully for the people and things that matter most.
- D. My health is in service of the people I love and the life I'm building with them. They're not separate from each other.

---

**Q11.**
The external thing driving your health habits is gone. The health scare resolved, the event passed, the urgency lifted. What happens to your habits?

- A. They quietly disappear. I didn't realize how much that pressure was holding things together.
- B. They get harder. I know I should keep going but the drive isn't there.
- C. They slow down but don't stop. My reason goes deeper than that one thing.
- D. They stay mostly intact. What's driving me runs deeper than the one thing that started it.

---

**Q12.**
You're going through the motions. Doing the habits, but the meaning behind them is completely gone. What do you do?

- A. Wait for the feeling to come back. Or don't, and things quietly drift.
- B. Try something new. Different workout, new approach, hoping it sparks something.
- C. Sit with it and try to reconnect with what originally made this matter.
- D. Remind myself the feeling isn't the point. Showing up is the point.
