## Page 4 — Your Action Plan

**What you need**

All three pieces. The fundamentals that actually work. A system flexible enough to survive your real life. And a reason deep enough to carry you through the weeks when nothing feels worth it.

You can't build all three at once. Most people who try end up back at the Monday restart cycle within a month. The fix is to start with one piece and let the others follow once the first is steady.

---

**Your action steps**

**1. Pick one pillar to start with this month. Not three.**

For most Restarters, Practice is the right place to start. Not because it's the most important, but because it's the one that holds the others up. A flexible system gives you somewhere to put new evidence (Proof) and somewhere to anchor a deeper reason (Purpose). Without Practice, the other two have nothing to land on.

If you've tried Practice-first before and it didn't hold, ask yourself why. If it broke because you didn't know what to do, start with Proof. If it broke because you didn't care enough to keep going, start with Purpose.

**2. Pick one specific small action this week.**

The single biggest mistake Restarters make is starting with too much. The all-or-nothing instinct says "if I'm going to do this, I have to really do it." The opposite is true. The smaller the start, the more likely it holds.

A few examples by pillar:

*Practice:* Pick one meal you already eat 3+ times a week. Replace half the plate with vegetables for the next 7 days. That's it. Don't change breakfast, don't add workouts, don't track anything.

*Proof:* Learn one of the settled fundamentals from a trustworthy source. Not the contested, headline-grabbing stuff. The basics that aren't actually in dispute, like why fiber matters or why sleep isn't optional. You don't need to research these. You need to learn them from someone who isn't selling you anything. My newsletter is built for exactly this.

*Purpose:* Write one sentence. "My health matters because ______." Don't overthink it. The first honest answer is usually closer to true than the polished version. You'll refine it over time.

**3. Know when you're ready to add the second pillar.**

The trap is adding too soon. The second pillar comes in when the first one has held for at least 4 weeks without breaking. Not 4 perfect weeks. 4 weeks where you adjusted instead of quitting.

Signs the first pillar is steady:

- A hard day no longer wipes out the habit
- You can describe what you're doing without referencing a plan or program
- The habit feels like something you do, not something you're trying

When those three are true, add the second piece.
